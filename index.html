A Deep Dive Into the Success Behind the San Antonio Spurs



Head Coach: Gregg Popovich

Arena/Stadium: AT&T Center

Location: San Antonio, Texas

Championships: 5 (1999, 2003, 2005, 2007, 2014)

Background

Since beating the New York Knicks in the 1999 NBA Finals, the San Antonio Spurs have amassed a regular season record of 969-383. Since then, they have made the Western Conference finals eight times, advanced to the NBA Finals five times, and won four more NBA championships. No other team in the NBA has had nearly as impressive a stretch, yet San Antonio remains one of the most underappreciated teams in all of professional sports. As a result, we want to explore the key factors behind their outstanding success.

Gregg Popovich took the reigns of the Spurs organization at the start of the 1996-1997 season. Only two short seasons later, the team, headlined by a Tim Duncan and David Robinson frontcourt, was able to claim the coveted Larry O'Brien Trophy for the first time. Popovich's basketball expertise permeates outside of the basketball court, serving as a key piece in personnel decision-making. He is said to have heavily influenced the selections of Tony Parker and Manu Ginobili, two role players that excelled at their positions in the 2005, 2007, and 2014. Moreover, he pushed for the draft night move that landed the Spurs future 2014-2015/2015-2016 Defensive Player of the Year and 2013-2014 Finals MVP, Kawhi Leonard, who is highly regarded as one of the best two-way players in the league. San Antonio was never a flashy free agent destination, yet their play is on par with that of a superteam; their systematic and patient way of playing the game is what contributed to their success in the 2000's. In order to achieve such dynastic prowess, it is essential to have a full roster that "buys in." History tells us, Pop did just that.

Success Factor #2: Consistent Results

Getting it done

In 42 seasons since joining the NBA, the Spurs have achieved a winning record 36 times. As of the end of 2021, the Spurs owned the NBA's all-time best win percentage; the team had won 62.2 percent of its games since joining the NBA, placing it ahead of teams like the Los Angeles Lakers and the Boston Celtics. The Spurs have won a total of 2,469 times, with 2,247 of those wins coming from the regular season and 222 from the playoffs.

Success Factor #4: Solid Points-Assists Ratio

Teamwork makes the dream work

Let's take a deeper look into offensive schemes; The San Antonio Spurs have been praised for their selfless offense which minimizes dribbles and prioritizes the extra pass. Because of their possessive style of play, the Spurs are able to convert far more than the rest of the league.

Speaking of a possessive style of play, the San Antonio Spurs have become increasingly keen on shooting the most efficient shots possible. In other words, they've become aware of the advantage of that consistent 2-point conversions hold. This is especially interesting in today's game, which favors scoring as many points as quickly as possible. On average, the Spurs are scoring every other 2-point attempt.

Success Factor #5: Defense Wins Championships

Balance is key

One of the greatest difficulties in constructing a roster is team fit. What players will complement the system set in place by the head coach? How will said system prioritize offense over defense, or vice versa? The San Antonio Spurs have done an exceptional job in establishing an elite defense while maintaining a historically efficient offense. Only the Miami Heat has a lower opponent field goal percentage at 0.442.
